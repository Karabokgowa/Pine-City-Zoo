ELEPHANTS

Elephants, the largest land mammals, are easily recognizable by their massive size, large ears, and long trunks. They are found in Africa and Asia, with two main species: the African elephant (including savanna and forest elephants) and the Asian elephant. African elephants have larger ears and longer front legs than hind legs, while Asian elephants have smaller ears, a more convex back, and a dish-shaped forehead. Key Features of Elephants: Size and Weight: African savanna elephants are the largest, with adult males reaching heights of 3 meters and weights of up to 6,000 kg. Tusks: Both male and female African elephants have tusks, while only some male Asian elephants have them. Trunk: The trunk is a versatile organ used for smelling, feeding, bathing, and even lifting objects. Ears: Large ears help elephants regulate their body temperature. Social Structure: Elephants live in family groups led by a female matriarch, and they form complex social bonds. Intelligence: Elephants are known for their intelligence and ability to exhibit complex behaviors, including grief and learning.
